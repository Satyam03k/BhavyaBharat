Streets of Flavor

A vibrant, fast-paced culinary journey through the bustling streets and savory bites of the Indian subcontinent.

Explore by Region

From spicy chaats to crispy bites, Indian street food is a spectacular explosion of taste. Discover the iconic snacks that keep the streets alive in the North, South, East, and West.

North India

Samosa

Speciality: A flaky, golden-fried triangular pastry stuffed with a warm, comforting mix of spiced mashed potatoes and peas. It is always served piping hot alongside sweet tamarind and spicy mint chutneys.

State: North India
North India

Aloo Tikki Chaat

Speciality: Crispy, golden potato patties smashed and generously topped with sweet tamarind chutney, spicy green mint chutney, and cool whisked yogurt.

State: Delhi / Uttar Pradesh
North India

Pani Puri / Golgappa

Speciality: Hollow, crispy puris filled to the brim with a mixture of fiercely spiced mint water, sweet tamarind, mashed potatoes, and chickpeas.

State: North India
South India

Kothu Parotta

Speciality: Shredded, flaky parotta flatbread stir-fried on a hot cast-iron griddle with egg, meat, onions, and a deeply spiced local gravy (salna).

State: Tamil Nadu
South India

Mirchi Bajji

Speciality: Large, mildly spicy green chilies stuffed with a tangy tamarind mixture, dipped in a thick gram flour batter, and deep-fried until perfectly crisp.

State: Andhra Pradesh
West India

Vada Pav

Speciality: The ultimate street food icon! A heavily spiced, deep-fried potato dumpling (vada) squished inside a soft bread bun (pav) with dry garlic chutney.

State: Maharashtra
West India

Dabeli

Speciality: A sweet, tangy, and spicy potato mixture stuffed inside a bun, richly garnished with fresh pomegranate seeds, roasted peanuts, and crunchy sev.

State: Gujarat
East India

Jhalmuri

Speciality: An instantly tossed, highly addictive snack made of puffed rice, mustard oil, chopped onions, green chilies, and a proprietary dry spice blend.

State: West Bengal
East India

Kathi Roll

Speciality: Skewered, coal-roasted kebabs (meat or paneer) wrapped snugly in a flaky paratha flatbread, layered with sliced onions, lime juice, and tangy sauces.

State: West Bengal
