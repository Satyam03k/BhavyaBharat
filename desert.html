Sweet Traditions

A delightful journey through the rich, decadent, and syrup-soaked world of Indian mithai (sweets).

Explore by Region

Every region in India has its own unique way of satisfying a sweet tooth. Discover the iconic desserts that complete a traditional Indian meal.

East India

Jalebi

Speciality:Crispy, bright orange spirals of deep-fried batter soaked in warm, saffron-infused sugar syrup. Every bite offers a sticky-sweet crunch that bursts with fragrant syrup.

State: North India
North India

Gulab Jamun

Speciality: Soft, melt-in-your-mouth deep-fried dough balls made from milk solids (khoya), soaked overnight in a warm, sticky, rose-scented sugar syrup.

State: North India
North India

Gajar Ka Halwa

Speciality: A rich, slow-cooked winter pudding made by simmering freshly grated carrots in whole milk, ghee, and sugar, generously garnished with almonds and pistachios.

State: Punjab
South India

Mysore Pak

Speciality: A legendary, decadent fudge-like sweet originally created in the royal kitchens of Mysore, made simply from generous amounts of pure ghee, sugar, and gram flour.

State: Karnataka
South India

Payasam

Speciality: A festive, creamy pudding made by boiling rice, vermicelli, or lentils with milk and jaggery. It is beautifully flavored with cardamom, roasted cashews, and raisins.

State: Kerala / Tamil Nadu
West India

Shrikhand

Speciality: A luxuriously thick and creamy dessert made from strained yogurt (chakka). It is heavily flavored with saffron, cardamom, and crushed pistachios, often served with hot puris.

State: Gujarat / Maharashtra
West India

Modak

Speciality: Delicate, sweet dumplings made from a soft rice flour shell, stuffed with a heavenly mixture of freshly grated coconut and melted jaggery, traditionally steamed to perfection.

State: Maharashtra
East India

Rasgulla

Speciality: Snow-white, incredibly spongy, and squeaky-clean dumplings made from fresh cottage cheese (chhena), boiled until light and airy in a clear, sweet sugar syrup.

State: West Bengal / Odisha
East India

Mishti Doi

Speciality: A classic fermented sweet yogurt set in traditional earthen clay pots. It is made by thickening milk and sweetening it with rich, caramelized date palm jaggery.

State: West Bengal
