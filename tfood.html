Flavors of India

A visual culinary journey through the rich heritage and traditional spices of the Indian subcontinent.

Explore by Region

Indian cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors. Discover iconic dishes that define the culinary landscape of the North, South, East, and West.

North India

Chole Bhature

Speciality: A fiery, spiced chickpea curry (Chole) paired with fluffy, deep-fried leavened bread (Bhature). A staple comfort food and popular street breakfast.

State: Punjab / Delhi
North India

Rogan Josh

Speciality: An aromatic lamb curry cooked with a gravy based on browned onions, yogurt, garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices like cloves and cardamom.

State: Kashmir
South India

Masala Dosa

Speciality: A thin, crispy crepe made from a fermented rice and lentil batter, wrapped around a savory spiced potato filling. Served with coconut chutney and sambar.

State: Karnataka
South India

Hyderabadi Biryani

Speciality: A world-famous dish made with basmati rice, meat, yogurt, and rich spices. Cooked using the slow "dum" method to lock in aroma.

State: Telangana
West India

Dhokla

Speciality: A savory sponge dish derived from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is steamed, making it light and tangy. Tempered with mustard seeds and curry leaves.

State: Gujarat
West India

Pav Bhaji

Speciality: A thick, spicy vegetable mash (Bhaji) garnished with coriander and onions, served hot with soft, buttered bread rolls (Pav).

State: Maharashtra
West India

Matar Paneer

Speciality:A classic, comforting North Indian curry featuring soft cubes of paneer and sweet green peas simmered in a rich, spiced tomato and onion gravy. It is perfectly balanced with a touch of cream and aromatic garam masala.

State: North India
East India

Macher Jhol

Speciality: A traditional, spicy fish stew cooked with turmeric, onions, garlic, and mustard oil. Heavily seasoned and served hot with steamed rice.

State: West Bengal
East India

Litti Chokha

Speciality: Whole wheat dough balls stuffed with Sattu (roasted chickpea flour), baked over coal, dipped in ghee, and served with mashed spicy vegetables (Chokha).

State: Bihar
